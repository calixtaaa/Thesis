================================================================================
DIY CONTINUITY TESTER CHECKLIST
Vending Machine Hardware - Complete Connection Verification
================================================================================

EQUIPMENT NEEDED:
- LED + 470Ω resistor connected to Pi 3.3V (one end) and Pi GND (other end)
- Test leads / multimeter probes
- Power OFF the entire system before testing (to avoid false readings)

TEST METHODOLOGY:
- Connect one test lead to 3.3V side of LED circuit
- Connect other test lead to GND side of LED circuit
- Touch probe A and B to test points
- LED illuminates = SHORT CIRCUIT (continuous path)
- LED dark = OPEN CIRCUIT (no continuous path)

================================================================================
SECTION 1: POWER RAILS (Should NOT light)
================================================================================
Power rail isolation is critical. These should be OPEN unless there's a decoupling
capacitor (which may show very slight glow).

Test 1.1: Pi 3.3V Rail to Common GND
├─ Test Points: Pi 3.3V (Pin 1) ↔ Pi GND (Pin 6)
├─ Expected: LED OFF (open circuit)
├─ Failure indicates: Short circuit in Pi power delivery or damaged capacitor
└─ Action: Power down immediately, check for burnt components

Test 1.2: Pi 5V Rail to Common GND
├─ Test Points: Pi 5V (Pin 2 or 4) ↔ Pi GND (Pin 6)
├─ Expected: LED OFF (open circuit)
├─ Failure indicates: Short in 5V supply or damaged component
└─ Action: Power down immediately, inspect for damage

Test 1.3: External 12V PSU+ to PSU GND
├─ Test Points: External 12V positive ↔ External 12V GND
├─ Expected: LED OFF (open circuit, external supply isolated)
├─ Failure indicates: Damage to external PSU or internal short
└─ Action: Check external PSU separately

SECTION 2: GROUND DISTRIBUTION (Should LIGHT)
================================================================================
All GND pins should be electrically connected to each other through the common
ground plane. These tests verify proper GND continuity.

Test 2.1: Pi GND Pin 6 ↔ Pi GND Pin 9
├─ Test Points: Pin 6 (GND) ↔ Pin 9 (GND)
├─ Expected: LED ON (continuous GND plane)
└─ Failure indicates: Broken GND trace or cold solder joint

Test 2.2: Pi GND Pin 6 ↔ Pi GND Pin 14
├─ Test Points: Pin 6 (GND) ↔ Pin 14 (GND)
├─ Expected: LED ON (continuous GND plane)
└─ Failure indicates: Broken GND trace or cold solder joint

Test 2.3: Pi GND Pin 6 ↔ External PSU GND
├─ Test Points: Pi GND (Pin 6) ↔ External 12V PSU GND
├─ Expected: LED ON (common ground reference)
├─ Failure indicates: Missing or disconnected GND jumper cable
└─ Action: Verify "common ground" jumper wire is soldered to both

Test 2.4: ULN2003 GND ↔ Pi Common GND
├─ Test Points: ULN2003 board GND ↔ Pi GND (Pin 6)
├─ Expected: LED ON (common ground)
└─ Failure indicates: Missing GND wire to ULN2003

================================================================================
QUICK REFERENCE SUMMARY
================================================================================

✓ SHOULD LIGHT (LED ON) - Continuous Path Expected:
  - All GND to GND connections
  - All power rail to device connections (3.3V, 5V, 12V)
  - All GPIO to target device connections
  - All I2C/SPI bus connections within same domain

✗ SHOULD NOT LIGHT (LED OFF) - Open Circuit Expected:
  - Power rail to different power rail (3.3V to 5V, 5V to 12V, etc.)
  - Different GPIO output pins
  - Different signal lines (MOSI ↔ MISO, SDA ↔ SCL, etc.)

⚠ AMBIGUOUS (May be Dim or Flickering):
  - I2C bus lines (due to pull-up resistors)
  - Input pins with internal pull-ups
  - Weak signals through capacitors
  (These are NORMAL; indicates resistive path, not a short)


================================================================================
TEST EXECUTION ORDER RECOMMENDATION
================================================================================

1. Power OFF all systems completely
2. Start with Section 1 (Power Rails) - verify NO shorts
3. Move to Section 2 (Ground Distribution) - verify ALL connected
4. Test Sections 3-9 systematically (bus connectivity)
5. Run Section 10 (Isolation) - verify domains separated
6. Run Section 11 (Signal Integrity) - final verification

Total estimated time: 30-45 minutes for complete checkout


================================================================================
TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE
================================================================================

LED Too Dim (Partial Light):
  → Likely cause: High-impedance path (pull-up resistor, capacitive coupling)
  → Action: Normal for I2C/inputs; check resistance with multimeter if concerned

LED Flickering:
  → Likely cause: Intermittent connection, corrosion, or vibration
  → Action: Reseat connectors, clean solder joints, check for cold solder

LED Won't Light When It Should:
  → Likely cause: Broken trace, loose connection, or cold solder joint
  → Action: Check solder joints under magnification, trace wiring carefully

Unexpected Short Found:
  → Action: Power down, photograph circuit, inspect for bridged solder
  → Safety: Do NOT apply power until short is isolated and fixed

Multiple Failures in Same Area:
  → Likely cause: Water damage, corrosion, or damaged IC
  → Action: Dry thoroughly, inspect for burn marks, replace damaged components
